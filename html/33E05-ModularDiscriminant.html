modular discriminant


Definition 1.

Let Λ⊂C be a lattice.

  1. 1.

    Let qτ=e2⁢π⁢i⁢τ. The Dedekind eta function is defined to be

    η⁢(τ)=qτ1/24⁢∏n=1∞(1-qτn)

    The Dedekind eta function should not be confused with the Weierstrass eta function, η⁢(w;Λ).

  2. 2.

    The j-invariant, as a function of lattices, is defined to be:

    j⁢(Λ)=g23g23-27⁢g32

    where g2 and g3 are certain multiples of the Eisenstein series of weight 4 and 6 (see http://planetmath.org/encyclopedia/ExamplesOfEllipticFunctions.htmlthis entry).

  3. 3.

    The Δ function (delta function or modular discriminant) is defined to be

    Δ⁢(Λ)=g23-27⁢g32

    Let Λτ be the lattice generated by 1,τ. The Δ function for Λτ has a product expansion

    Δ⁢(τ)=Δ⁢(Λτ)=(2⁢π⁢i)12⁢qτ⁢∏n=1∞(1-qτn)24=(2⁢π⁢i)12⁢η⁢(τ)24
