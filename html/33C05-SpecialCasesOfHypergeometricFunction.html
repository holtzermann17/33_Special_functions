special cases of hypergeometric function


Many elementary (http://planetmath.org/ElementaryFunction) and non-elementary transcendental functions may be expressed as special cases of the hypergeometric functions

F⁢(a,b,c;x)= 1+a⁢b1!⁢c⁢x+a⁢(a+1)⁢b⁢(b+1)2!⁢c⁢(c+1)⁢x2+a⁢(a+1)⁢(a+2)⁢b⁢(b+1)⁢(b+2)3!⁢c⁢(c+1)⁢(c+2)⁢x3+…,

which are solutions of the hypergeometric equation

x⁢(x-1)⁢d2⁢yd⁢x2+(c-(a+b+1))⁢d⁢yd⁢x-a⁢b⁢y= 0.

For example:

  • •

    (1+x)n=F⁢(-n, 1, 1;-x)

  • •

    ln⁡(1+x)=x⁢F⁢(1, 1, 2;-x)

  • •

    ln⁡1+x1-x= 2⁢x⁢F⁢(12, 1,32;x2)

  • •

    arcsin⁡x=x⁢F⁢(12,12,32;x2)

  • •

    arctan⁡x=x⁢F⁢(12, 1,32;-x2)

  • •

    sin⁡(m⁢arcsin⁡x)=m⁢x⁢F⁢(1+m2,1-m2,32;x2)

  • •

    cos⁡(m⁢arcsin⁡x)=F⁢(m2,-m2,12;x2)

  • •

    Tn⁢(x)=F⁢(n,-n,12;1-x2)  (Chebyshev polynomials)

  • •

    Pn⁢(x)=F⁢(-n,n+1, 1;1-x2)  (Legendre polynomials)

  • •

    ∫0π2d⁢φ1-x2⁢sin2⁡φ =π2⁢F⁢(12,12, 1;x2)  (complete elliptic integral of 1st kind)

  • •

    ∫0π21-x2⁢sin2⁡φ⁢𝑑φ =π2⁢F⁢(-12,12, 1;x2)  (complete elliptic integral of 2nd kind)
